Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us

Last Updated: February 5, 2026 | Effective Date: February 5, 2026

1. Introduction

SlideTheory ("we," "us," "our," or "SlideTheory") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our AI-powered presentation slide generation platform and related services (collectively, the "Services").

This Privacy Policy complies with:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) - European Union
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) / California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
  • Other applicable privacy laws

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

Account Information:

  • Name and email address
  • Password (encrypted)
  • Company/organization name (optional)
  • Job title (optional)
  • Profile picture (optional)

Payment Information:

  • Credit card details (processed by Stripe - we do not store full card numbers)
  • Billing address
  • Transaction history

User Content:

  • Text inputs provided for slide generation
  • Uploaded files (CSV, TXT)
  • Generated slides and presentations
  • Templates and customizations

Communications:

  • Support requests and correspondence
  • Survey responses
  • Feedback and suggestions

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

Usage Data:

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Referring website
  • Pages visited and features used
  • Time spent on platform
  • Click patterns and interactions

AI Processing Data:

  • Prompts submitted for slide generation
  • Generated outputs
  • Selection and modification history
  • Error logs and performance metrics

Cookies and Similar Technologies:

  • Session cookies (essential functionality)
  • Preference cookies (settings and customization)
  • Analytics cookies (usage patterns)
  • Advertising cookies (limited, opt-in only)

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Authentication providers (if using social login)
  • Payment processors (transaction confirmations)
  • Analytics providers (aggregated usage data)

7. Your Privacy Rights

7.1 GDPR Rights (EU/EEA Users)

You have the right to:

  1. Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  2. Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  3. Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your data
  4. Restriction: Limit how we process your data
  5. Data Portability: Receive data in a structured, machine-readable format
  6. Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  7. Withdraw Consent: Revoke consent at any time
  8. Automated Decision-Making: Not be subject to solely automated decisions with significant effects

7.2 CCPA/CPRA Rights (California Residents)

You have the right to:

  1. Know: Request disclosure of categories and specific pieces of personal information collected
  2. Delete: Request deletion of personal information
  3. Opt-Out: Opt-out of sale/sharing of personal information
  4. Non-Discrimination: Not be discriminated against for exercising privacy rights
  5. Correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information
  6. Limit: Limit use and disclosure of sensitive personal information

7.3 How to Exercise Your Rights

Contact Information

Email: privacy@slidetheory.com

Account Settings: Available in your profile dashboard

We will respond within:

  • GDPR: 30 days (may extend to 60 with notification)
  • CCPA: 45 days (may extend to 90 with notification)

Verification: We will verify your identity before processing requests to protect your privacy.

8. Data Security

8.1 Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures:

  • Encryption: AES-256 for data at rest, TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • Access Controls: Role-based access, multi-factor authentication
  • Monitoring: 24/7 security monitoring and intrusion detection
  • Backups: Encrypted, geographically distributed backups
  • Employee Training: Regular security awareness training
  • Third-Party Audits: Annual security assessments

8.2 Data Breach Notification

In case of a data breach:

  • We will notify affected users within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)
  • We will notify relevant supervisory authorities as required
  • We will provide guidance on protective measures
