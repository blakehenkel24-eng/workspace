# SlideTheory Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

## Overview
Understanding the underlying jobs users hire SlideTheory to do, organized by the Forces of Progress framework.

---

## Core JTBD Statement

**When** I need to communicate ideas visually to drive action,
**I want to** create compelling presentations efficiently,
**So I can** achieve my professional objectives without getting bogged down in design details.

---

## Job Map: Create a Presentation

### Functional Jobs

```
1. DEFINE THE STORY
   ├── Understand the audience
   ├── Clarify the objective
   ├── Structure the narrative
   └── Define key messages

2. GATHER CONTENT
   ├── Collect existing materials
   ├── Research and validate
   ├── Draft copy and data
   └── Curate supporting visuals

3. DESIGN THE DECK
   ├── Select/create template
   ├── Apply brand guidelines
   ├── Layout content visually
   └── Refine aesthetics

4. COLLABORATE & REFINE
   ├── Share for feedback
   ├── Incorporate revisions
   ├── Align with stakeholders
   └── Finalize content

5. DELIVER & DISTRIBUTE
   ├── Export in right format
   ├── Present (live or async)
   ├── Distribute to audience
   └── Track engagement

6. ITERATE & REUSE
   ├── Gather learnings
   ├── Update for next use
   ├── Build reusable assets
   └── Maintain version history
```

### Emotional Jobs

**Personal:**
- Feel confident presenting my ideas
- Avoid embarrassment in front of audience
- Demonstrate competence and professionalism
- Express my creativity

**Social:**
- Impress colleagues and superiors
- Align my team around a vision
- Win approval/buy-in from stakeholders
- Establish credibility and authority

### Supporting Jobs

- Learn presentation best practices
- Manage time effectively
- Coordinate with team members
- Maintain brand consistency
- Store and organize assets

## The Forces of Progress

### Forces at Play for Each Job

```
PUSH: Circumstance causing desire for change
     ↓
PULL: Attraction of new solution
     ↓
ANXIETY: Concerns about switching
     ↓
HABIT: Comfort with current approach
```

---

## JTBD 1: Create Pitch Deck (Startup Sarah)

### Push Forces
- Investor meeting scheduled with little notice
- Current deck looks amateurish
- Designer unavailable or unaffordable
- Running out of time before meeting
- Previous pitch didn't land well

### Pull Forces
- See examples of great pitch decks
- Promise of AI-generated professional designs
- Fast turnaround time
- Affordable compared to designer
- Built-in best practices

### Anxieties
- Will it look generic/AI-generated?
- Can I customize enough for my brand?
- Is the quality really professional?
- Will investors notice it's template-based?
- What if the AI suggests wrong content?

### Habits
- Using familiar PowerPoint/Google Slides
- Asking designer friend for help
- Spending all-nighters polishing slides
- Accepting "good enough" quality

### Purchase Criteria
1. Quality of output (must look professional)
2. Speed of creation (hours, not days)
3. Customization options (brand alignment)
4. Price (startup budget)
5. Export flexibility (PDF, web, etc.)

## Competitive Positioning by JTBD

### vs. PowerPoint/Google Slides
- **Ours:** Speed and design quality out of the box
- **Theirs:** Familiarity and enterprise ubiquity
- **Win on:** Users who value time and don't need deep customization

### vs. Canva
- **Ours:** Presentation-specific workflows and structure
- **Theirs:** Broad design capabilities and template variety
- **Win on:** Professional/business presentations vs. general design

### vs. Beautiful.ai
- **Ours:** AI-powered content generation
- **Theirs:** Established smart template system
- **Win on:** Content intelligence, not just layout

### vs. Gamma/Tome
- **Ours:** Traditional deck format, familiar output
- **Theirs:** Novel narrative/web-first formats
- **Win on:** Users who need standard presentations
