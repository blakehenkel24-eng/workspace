# Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Last Updated:** February 5, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between SlideTheory, Inc. ("Processor," "we," "us," or "our") and the user ("Controller," "you," or "your") and applies when SlideTheory processes personal data on your behalf.

## 1. Definitions

**"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means:
- General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)
- UK Data Protection Act 2018 and UK GDPR
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)
- Any other applicable data protection legislation

**"Controller"** means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data.

**"Data Subject"** means the identified or identifiable natural person to whom personal data relates.

**"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.

**"Processing"** means any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.

**"Processor"** means SlideTheory, Inc., the entity processing personal data on behalf of the Controller.

**"Subprocessor"** means any third party engaged by the Processor to process personal data.

## 2. Scope and Application

### 2.1 When This DPA Applies
This DPA applies when:
- You are a business customer using SlideTheory's Services
- You upload or process personal data of Data Subjects through our platform
- You are subject to GDPR, CCPA, or similar data protection laws

### 2.2 Relationship to Terms of Service
This DPA supplements the Terms of Service. In case of conflict, this DPA governs regarding data protection matters.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

### 3.1 Controller Responsibilities
As Controller, you:
- Determine the purposes and means of processing
- Ensure lawful basis for processing exists
- Provide required notices to Data Subjects
- Honor Data Subject rights requests
- Maintain records of processing activities
- Ensure data minimization and accuracy

### 3.2 Processor Responsibilities
As Processor, SlideTheory will:
- Process personal data only on documented instructions from you
- Ensure persons authorized to process are bound by confidentiality
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Assist you in responding to Data Subject requests
- Assist with data protection impact assessments
- Notify you of personal data breaches without undue delay
- Delete or return personal data at your request
- Make available information necessary for compliance audits

## 4. Processing Details

### 4.1 Subject Matter
Processing of personal data submitted by or on behalf of the Controller through SlideTheory's AI-powered presentation generation platform.

### 4.2 Duration
Processing continues for the duration of your Subscription or until termination of the Terms of Service, plus any retention period required by law.

### 4.3 Nature and Purpose
| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Providing slide generation services, account management, customer support, and service improvement |
| **Nature** | Collection, storage, processing, transmission, and deletion of personal data |
| **Data Subjects** | End users, employees, customers, or individuals whose data you include in presentations |
| **Categories** | Contact information, professional information, content uploaded for processing |

### 4.4 Types of Personal Data
- **Contact Data:** Names, email addresses, phone numbers
- **Professional Data:** Job titles, company names, department information
- **User Content:** Any personal data included in uploaded files or prompts
- **Account Data:** Login credentials, preferences, usage history

### 4.5 Special Categories
We do not intentionally process special category data (racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic/biometric data, health data, sex life/orientation). If you upload such data, you must ensure appropriate legal basis and safeguards.

## 5. Processor Instructions

SlideTheory is instructed to process personal data:
1. **As necessary** to provide the Services under the Terms of Service
2. **As documented** in this DPA and Privacy Policy
3. **As required** by applicable law (in which case we will notify you, unless prohibited)
4. **For security** purposes to protect our systems and users
5. **For improvement** of our AI models (anonymized data only)

## 7. Data Subject Rights

### 7.1 Assistance with Requests
SlideTheory will assist you in responding to Data Subject requests:
- Access requests: Provide data export capabilities
- Rectification: Enable data correction in account settings
- Erasure: Delete data upon verified request
- Restriction: Suspend processing as directed
- Portability: Provide data in structured format
- Objection: Cease processing where applicable

### 7.2 Direct Requests
If a Data Subject contacts us directly:
- We will forward the request to you within 48 hours
- We will not respond directly unless authorized by you
- We will provide our cooperation to fulfill the request

### 7.3 Response Timeframes
We commit to assisting with Data Subject requests within:
- Standard requests: 5 business days
- Urgent requests: 48 hours
- Complex requests: As agreed with you

## 8. Security Measures

### 8.1 Technical Measures
- Encryption of data at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
- Access controls and authentication mechanisms
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing
- Intrusion detection and monitoring systems
- Secure development practices

### 8.2 Organizational Measures
- Data protection officer appointment
- Staff training on data protection
- Incident response procedures
- Regular policy reviews and updates
- Vendor security assessments

### 8.3 Security Standards
SlideTheory maintains security measures meeting or exceeding:
- ISO 27001 standards
- SOC 2 Type II requirements
- GDPR Article 32 requirements
- Industry best practices

## 9. Personal Data Breaches

### 9.1 Breach Notification
In case of a personal data breach:
- We will notify you without undue delay (within 24 hours of discovery)
- Notification will include: nature of breach, categories affected, likely consequences, measures taken
- We will cooperate with your breach notification obligations to authorities and Data Subjects

### 9.2 Investigation and Remediation
- We will investigate the root cause
- We will implement measures to prevent recurrence
- We will document all breaches and responses

### 11.2 Audit Scope
Audits may cover:
- Documentation of processing activities
- Security measures and certifications
- Subprocessor agreements
- Incident response records
- Data retention and deletion practices

### 11.3 Alternative to Audit
We may provide:
- SOC 2 Type II reports
- ISO 27001 certificates
- Third-party security assessments
- In lieu of direct audit
