- **Green Gaps:** Features SlideTheory has that competitors lack (advantages)

## Competitor-by-Competitor Gap Analysis

### vs. Beautiful.ai

**Where They Win:**
- 300+ Smart Slide layouts
- Enterprise security (SOC 2, SSO)
- Live data linking to charts
- Audio/video recording per slide
- Version control & locked slides

**Where We Can Win:**
- Simpler pricing (no $40/user for teams)
- Faster time-to-first-deck
- More modern UI
- Better free tier

**Strategic Response:**
- Focus on "Beautiful.ai without the enterprise bloat"
- Target SMBs priced out of Beautiful.ai Team

---

### vs. Gamma

**Where They Win:**
- Advanced customization settings
- Web page/document generation (not just presentations)
- Strong embedding capabilities
- Notion integration
- Widget framework

**Where We Can Win:**
- Presentation focus (Gamma spreads thin)
- Faster generation workflow
- Simpler UX (Gamma has feature creep)

**Strategic Response:**
- Double down on presentation-specific features
- Build embedding capabilities to match

---

### vs. Tome

**Where They Win:**
- Visual storytelling focus
- Figma integration
- Creative/professional aesthetic
- Strong with designer audience

**Where We Can Win:**
- Business/enterprise features
- Better data/chart support
- More professional templates

**Strategic Response:**
- Position as "Tome for business professionals"
- Add better data visualization

---

### vs. Canva

**Where They Win:**
- Massive ecosystem (social, video, print)
- 100M+ user network effects
- Best free tier in market
- Strong brand recognition
- Mobile apps

**Where We Can Win:**
- Presentation depth over breadth
- AI-native (vs. bolted-on)
- No design skill required
- Faster to professional results

**Strategic Response:**
- "Presentations only, done better"
- Focus on AI quality vs. template quantity

---

### vs. Pitch

**Where They Win:**
- Sales-specific workflows
- CRM integrations
- Data linking for live stats
- Advanced sharing rooms
- Strong analytics for sales

**Where We Can Win:**
- General purpose (not just sales)
- Better pricing (no 2-seat minimum)
- Easier for non-sales use cases

**Strategic Response:**
- Position as "Pitch for everyone else"
- Build sales features over time

## Recommendations

### 1. Close Critical Gaps First
Focus on real-time collaboration and PPTX export. These are deal-breakers for team adoption.

### 2. Choose Differentiation Battles Wisely
Don't compete with Canva on breadth or Pitch on sales features. Win on:
- Speed (faster than Gamma)
- Simplicity (easier than Beautiful.ai)
- Quality (better than SlidesAI)
- Price (cheaper than Beautiful.ai Team)

### 3. Build Defensible Moats
- Template marketplace (network effects)
- AI training on user data (proprietary models)
- Vertical expertise (industry-specific intelligence)

### 4. Monitor Emerging Gaps
Track competitors for:
- New AI capabilities
- Integration expansions
- Pricing changes
- Feature pivots
