# SlideTheory User Personas

## Overview
Detailed personas representing our core user segments to guide product decisions, marketing, and customer success.

---

## Persona 1: Startup Sarah

### Demographics
- **Age:** 26-34
- **Role:** Founder / CEO / Head of Growth
- **Company:** Early-stage startup (2-20 employees)
- **Location:** Urban tech hub (SF, NYC, London, Berlin)
- **Salary:** $80K-150K + equity

### Background
Sarah just raised a seed round and needs to create pitch decks for investors, product demos for customers, and team updates. She's technical but not a designer. Every hour spent on presentations is an hour not spent building product.

### Goals
1. Create investor-ready pitch decks quickly
2. Maintain consistent brand as company grows
3. Iterate rapidly based on feedback
4. Look professional without hiring a designer

### Pain Points
- "PowerPoint makes everything look amateur"
- "I spend 6 hours on slides that should take 1"
- "Designers are too expensive and slow"
- "Every deck looks slightly different"

### Behaviors
- Works late nights before investor meetings
- Iterates decks 10+ times based on feedback
- Shares via email and video calls
- Values speed over pixel-perfect design

### Quote
> "I need to look like a $100M company even though we're 5 people in a WeWork."

### Technology Profile
- **Primary tools:** Google Slides, Figma (for product), Notion
- **Device:** MacBook Pro, iPhone
- **Comfort level:** High with tech, impatient with friction
- **Collaboration:** Async via comments, real-time for urgent items

### JTBD
**When** I have an investor meeting coming up,
**I want to** create a compelling pitch deck quickly,
**So I can** focus on building the business instead of formatting slides.

### Success Metrics
- Time to first deck: < 30 minutes
- Investor conversion rate
- Brand consistency across materials

### Acquisition Channels
- Twitter/X (follows VCs and startup accounts)
- Product Hunt
- Y Combinator network
- Podcasts (How I Built This, Masters of Scale)

## Persona 2: Marketing Mike

### Demographics
- **Age:** 28-40
- **Role:** Marketing Manager / Demand Gen Lead
- **Company:** Mid-market SaaS (50-500 employees)
- **Location:** Hybrid/remote, major metro
- **Salary:** $90K-140K

### Background
Mike manages a portfolio of marketing campaigns and needs to create sales collateral, webinar decks, case studies, and internal reports. He works with sales, product, and design teams. Brand compliance is critical.

### Goals
1. Produce on-brand materials at scale
2. Enable sales team with great collateral
3. Measure content performance
4. Maintain brand consistency globally

### Pain Points
- "Sales reps keep messing up the brand colors"
- "I create the same deck type 20 times a month"
- "Version control is a nightmare"
- "Can't track if anyone actually uses what I make"

### Behaviors
- Uses templates religiously
- Collaborates with 5+ stakeholders per deck
- Maintains brand guidelines documentation
- Tracks deck performance through sales feedback

### Quote
> "I need my team to create brand-compliant decks without me checking every pixel."

### Technology Profile
- **Primary tools:** PowerPoint, Canva, Marketo, Salesforce
- **Device:** Windows laptop + iPad for presenting
- **Comfort level:** Moderate - learns tools that help the team
- **Collaboration:** Heavy - multiple review cycles

### JTBD
**When** sales needs new competitive battlecards,
**I want to** create on-brand materials they can customize,
**So I can** scale my impact without becoming a bottleneck.

### Success Metrics
- Time saved per deck
- Brand compliance score
- Sales satisfaction with materials
- Content reuse rate

### Acquisition Channels
- LinkedIn
- Marketing communities (Demand Curve, Reforge)
- Webinars
- G2/Capterra reviews

## Persona 3: Consultant Carla

### Demographics
- **Age:** 32-45
- **Role:** Independent Consultant / Freelancer
- **Company:** Self-employed or small firm (1-10 people)
- **Location:** Remote, travels to clients
- **Salary:** $120K-250K (variable)

### Background
Carla advises clients on strategy, operations, or digital transformation. Every engagement requires custom presentations. Her decks ARE her product - they demonstrate expertise and justify high fees.

### Goals
1. Create differentiated, high-quality deliverables
2. Maintain IP/reusability across clients
3. Work efficiently to maximize billable time
4. Impress clients with polish and insight

### Pain Points
- "Clients expect McKinsey quality on a tight budget"
- "I recreate frameworks for every project"
- "Hard to maintain my 'secret sauce' templates"
- "Need to work offline on planes"

### Behaviors
- Builds reusable frameworks and methodologies
- Customizes heavily for each client
- Presents in boardrooms and over Zoom
- Bills by project, not by hour (efficiency = profit)

### Quote
> "My slides are my reputation. One sloppy deck can cost me a referral."

### Technology Profile
- **Primary tools:** PowerPoint (deep expertise), Excel, Miro
- **Device:** Surface Pro or MacBook, presents via clicker
- **Comfort level:** Very high - power user
- **Collaboration:** Client-facing sharing, solo creation

### JTBD
**When** starting a new client engagement,
**I want to** quickly assemble a professional proposal deck,
**So I can** win the business and start delivering value faster.

### Success Metrics
- Win rate on proposals
- Client satisfaction scores
- Time to deliverable completion
- Referral rate

### Acquisition Channels
- LinkedIn thought leadership
- Industry conferences
- Referrals
- Podcast guest appearances

## Persona 4: Enterprise Eric

### Demographics
- **Age:** 35-50
- **Role:** Director / VP / Executive
- **Company:** Fortune 500 / Large enterprise (5000+ employees)
- **Location:** Corporate HQ + travel
- **Salary:** $180K-400K

### Background
Eric leads a division and presents to the C-suite, board, and external stakeholders. His presentations drive million-dollar decisions. He has an assistant and access to design resources but tight deadlines.

### Goals
1. Communicate complex information clearly
2. Drive alignment across large organizations
3. Look authoritative and data-driven
4. Move efficiently from concept to boardroom

### Pain Points
- "I have the data but can't tell the story visually"
- "My assistant isn't a designer"
- "Legal/compliance review slows everything"
- "Need to work with existing enterprise tools"

### Behaviors
- Delegates first draft, refines personally
- Data-heavy presentations
- Multiple review cycles with legal
- Presents to 10-100+ person audiences

### Quote
> "I don't have time to become a design expert. I need executive-ready output."

### Technology Profile
- **Primary tools:** PowerPoint, Excel, Tableau, Teams
- **Device:** Company-issued Windows laptop
- **Comfort level:** Moderate - uses tools provided
- **Collaboration:** Top-down, approval chains

### JTBD
**When** preparing for a board presentation,
**I want to** transform my data into a compelling narrative,
**So I can** secure budget approval and demonstrate results.

### Success Metrics
- Board approval rate
- Time to presentation-ready
- Stakeholder comprehension scores
- Executive presence ratings

### Acquisition Channels
- Enterprise sales outreach
- Industry analyst reports (Gartner)
- Peer referrals
- Conference sponsorships

## Persona 5: Educator Emma

### Demographics
- **Age:** 28-55
- **Role:** University Professor / Corporate Trainer / Online Course Creator
- **Company:** University or training organization
- **Location:** Campus/remote
- **Salary:** $60K-120K

### Background
Emma creates educational content for students or corporate learners. She needs engaging visuals that aid comprehension. Accessibility and clarity are priorities over flashy design.

### Goals
1. Create clear, accessible learning materials
2. Engage students with visual storytelling
3. Save time on repetitive lecture prep
4. Ensure accessibility compliance

### Pain Points
- "Students zone out during text-heavy slides"
- "Creating diagrams takes forever"
- "Need to accommodate visual impairments"
- "Updating materials every semester is tedious"

### Behaviors
- Reuses core content across semesters
- Focuses on clarity and pedagogy
- Shares via LMS (Canvas, Blackboard)
- Accessibility-first mindset

### Quote
> "My slides need to teach, not just inform. Visual clarity is everything."

### Technology Profile
- **Primary tools:** PowerPoint, Google Slides, Canvas, Zoom
- **Device:** Mixed - often institution-provided
- **Comfort level:** Moderate - learns tools that help students
- **Collaboration:** Limited - mostly solo creation

### JTBD
**When** preparing next week's lecture,
**I want to** create clear visual aids quickly,
**So my students can** understand complex concepts and stay engaged.

### Success Metrics
- Student engagement scores
- Comprehension test results
- Time saved on prep
- Accessibility audit scores

### Acquisition Channels
- Education conferences
- University IT recommendations
- LinkedIn learning communities
- Education technology blogs

## Persona 6: Designer Dana

### Demographics
- **Age:** 24-35
- **Role:** Graphic Designer / Brand Designer / Creative Lead
- **Company:** Agency or in-house design team
- **Location:** Urban creative hub
- **Salary:** $70K-120K

### Background
Dana creates presentations for clients or internal teams. She values creative control and precision. She may be skeptical of AI tools but interested in efficiency gains for repetitive work.

### Goals
1. Maintain creative control and precision
2. Speed up production work
3. Ensure brand consistency
4. Focus on high-value creative tasks

### Pain Points
- "Clients want 'just a quick deck' but expect custom design"
- "PowerPoint is limiting but clients require it"
- "Repetitive formatting kills creativity"
- "Hard to maintain design systems in slides"

### Behaviors
- Starts in Figma, adapts for presentation tools
- Pixel-perfect attention to detail
- Builds and maintains templates
- Educates clients on design value

### Quote
> "I need tools that respect design craft, not replace it."

### Technology Profile
- **Primary tools:** Figma, Adobe CC, Keynote, PowerPoint
- **Device:** MacBook Pro, external monitor, tablet
- **Comfort level:** Very high - expert user
- **Collaboration:** Client presentations, team critiques

### JTBD
**When** a client needs a presentation template system,
**I want to** build sophisticated, maintainable templates efficiently,
**So I can** deliver professional results within budget.

### Success Metrics
- Design quality scores
- Client satisfaction
- Time saved on production
- Template reuse rate

### Acquisition Channels
- Design communities (Dribbble, Behance)
- Design Twitter
- Creative conferences
- Design tool newsletters
