# SlideTheory User Interview Guide

## Purpose
Conduct structured, insightful interviews to understand user needs, pain points, and workflows around presentation creation.

---

## Interview Types

### 1. Discovery Interviews
**Goal:** Understand current presentation workflows and pain points
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Participants:** New signups, non-users in target segment

### 2. Usability Interviews
**Goal:** Identify friction points in the product
**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Participants:** Active users (various experience levels)

### 3. JTBD Interviews
**Goal:** Uncover jobs users hire presentations to do
**Duration:** 60-90 minutes
**Participants:** Mixed (users and non-users)

### 4. Churn Interviews
**Goal:** Understand why users stopped using SlideTheory
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes
**Participants:** Canceled/lapsed users

## Discovery Interview Script (60 min)

### Introduction (5 min)
```
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to chat today. I'm [Your Name] from SlideTheory. 
This conversation is all about understanding your experience with presentations - there are 
no wrong answers, and your honest feedback will help us build something genuinely useful.

A few quick things:
- This will take about an hour
- I'd love to record this for my notes - is that okay?
- Feel free to skip any questions
- Your feedback won't be shared outside our team

To start, can you tell me a bit about your role and what a typical week looks like for you?"
```

### Current State - Presentation Creation (15 min)

**Walk me through the last presentation you created.**
- What was it for?
- Who was the audience?
- What tools did you use?
- How long did it take?

**When you're starting a new presentation, what's your process?**
- Where do you begin?
- What resources do you gather first?
- Do you sketch/wireframe or go straight to the tool?

**What makes a presentation "good" in your context?**
- How do you know when it's ready?
- Who provides feedback?
- What's the approval process like?

### Pain Points & Frustrations (15 min)

**What's the most frustrating part of creating presentations for you?**
- Tell me about a time when things went wrong
- What took way longer than it should have?
- When have you felt embarrassed by a presentation?

**How do you handle design/branding consistency?**
- Do you have brand guidelines?
- How do you ensure compliance?
- Who checks for consistency?

**What about collaboration?**
- Who else touches your presentations?
- How do you share and get feedback?
- Version control nightmares?

### Switching & Alternatives (10 min)

**What tools have you tried for presentations?**
- Why did you choose your current tool?
- What made you leave previous tools?
- What's stopping you from trying something new?

**If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about presentations, what would it be?**
- Why that specifically?
- What would the ideal solution look like?

### Demo & Reaction (10 min)

*[Share screen - 5-min overview of SlideTheory]*

**Initial reaction:**
- What's your first impression?
- What resonates with your workflow?
- What feels unfamiliar or concerning?

**Specific scenarios:**
- "Imagine you had to create [their recent presentation] in this. What would that look like?"
- What would make you want to try this?
- What would make you hesitate?

### Closing (5 min)

**Final questions:**
- Who else should I talk to about this?
- Any questions for me about SlideTheory?
- Can I reach out if I have follow-up questions?

**Thank you & next steps:**
- "Really appreciate your time today, [Name]. Your insights are incredibly valuable."
- Offer incentive/gift card
- Schedule follow-up if appropriate

## JTBD Interview Script (90 min)

### The "Switch" Interview Method

**Focus:** Understanding the moment someone decides to create a presentation

### Timeline Exercise

Draw a timeline together:
```
[First Thought] ---- [Active Looking] ---- [Deciding] ---- [Purchasing/Using] ---- [Ongoing Use]
```

### Key Questions by Stage

**First Thought (10 min)**
- Tell me about the moment you realized you needed a presentation.
- What was happening in your work/life at that time?
- Who was involved in that realization?

**Active Looking (15 min)**
- What did you do next?
- What options did you consider?
- Who did you talk to for advice?
- What were you Googling?

**Deciding (15 min)**
- What made you choose [tool they picked]?
- What almost made you choose something else?
- What were the deal-breakers?

**Purchasing/Using (15 min)**
- Walk me through your first experience.
- Did reality match your expectations?
- What surprised you?

**Ongoing Use (15 min)**
- How has your usage evolved?
- What's become easier? What's still hard?
- If you stopped using it tomorrow, what would you miss most?

### The Forces Analysis

**PUSH** (from current situation)
- What wasn't working with your old approach?
- What was getting worse?

**PULL** (to new solution)
- What attracted you to [solution]?
- What did you imagine would be better?

**ANXIETY** (about switching)
- What were you worried about?
- What could have gone wrong?

**HABIT** (keeping you in place)
- What was comfortable about the old way?
- What would you have had to give up?

## Usability Interview Script (45 min)

### Tasks to Observe

**Task 1: Starting Fresh (10 min)**
"You need to create a presentation about Q4 sales results. Show me how you'd start."

*Observe:*
- Do they look for templates?
- Do they start from scratch?
- How do they handle content?

**Task 2: Working with AI (10 min)**
"You have a rough outline in a document. Use AI to help you build this presentation."

*Observe:*
- Do they trust the AI suggestions?
- How much do they edit vs. accept?
- Where do they get stuck?

**Task 3: Customizing Design (10 min)**
"This needs to match your company brand colors and fonts."

*Observe:*
- Can they find brand settings?
- Do they understand the hierarchy?
- How do they handle precision?

**Task 4: Collaboration (10 min)**
"Your manager needs to review this before tomorrow's meeting."

*Observe:*
- Sharing method chosen
- Permission settings
- Export preferences

**Task 5: Export & Present (5 min)**
"You're about to present this in a meeting."

*Observe:*
- Export format choice
- Presentation mode
- Confidence level

### Follow-up Questions After Each Task

- What was clear? What was confusing?
- Did anything surprise you?
- What would you expect to happen that didn't?
- Would you do this differently in your real workflow?

## Churn Interview Script (30 min)

### Opening
```
"I noticed you haven't used SlideTheory in a while, and I wanted to understand what happened. 
This isn't about convincing you to come back - I genuinely want to learn so we can improve."
```

### Questions

**The Moment of Leaving (10 min)**
- Walk me through your last session with SlideTheory.
- What were you trying to do?
- What happened that made you stop?

**Before That (10 min)**
- How was your overall experience before that moment?
- What was working well?
- What was consistently frustrating?

**After That (5 min)**
- What did you switch to?
- How is that working out?
- What do you miss about SlideTheory (if anything)?

**The Path Back (5 min)**
- What would need to change for you to try again?
- Any specific features that would make a difference?

## Probing Techniques

### The 5 Whys
When you hear a problem, dig deeper:
- "Why was that frustrating?"
- "Why did that matter?"
- "Why was that the case?"
- "Why was that important?"
- "Why did you feel that way?"

### Story Elicitation
- "Tell me about a specific time when..."
- "Walk me through exactly what happened..."
- "What were you thinking in that moment?"

### Contrast Questions
- "How is this different from...?"
- "What would you have done before [tool]?"
- "What's the best/worst presentation experience you've had?"

### Projection
- "Imagine it's 6 months from now..."
- "If you were teaching a new hire..."
- "If budget wasn't an issue..."

**Segment A: Design Agencies**
- 5+ presentations/week
- High design standards
- Collaboration-heavy

- Pitch decks
- Speed over polish
- DIY mentality

**Segment C: Enterprise PMs**
- Stakeholder updates
- Template-heavy
- Brand compliance

**Segment D: Freelance Consultants**

## Post-Interview Process

1. **Immediate (within 1 hour)**
   - Upload recording
   - Fill out note template
   - Tag key moments

2. **Same Day**
   - Send thank you + incentive
   - Add to research database
   - Flag urgent insights

3. **Weekly**
   - Synthesis with other interviews
   - Update personas/JTBD
   - Share insights with team

4. **Monthly**
   - Pattern analysis
   - Update interview guide
   - Adjust recruiting criteria
