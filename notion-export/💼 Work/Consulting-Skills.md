### McKinsey Frameworks

#### MECE Principle
> Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive

**What it is:** A structuring principle ensuring all options are covered without overlap.

**When to use:**
- Problem structuring
- Hypothesis generation
- Issue tree development
- Market segmentation

**Template:**
```
Problem: _______________________________

Branch 1: _______________
  - Sub-branch A
  - Sub-branch B

Branch 2: _______________
  - Sub-branch A
  - Sub-branch B

[Check: Are branches MECE?]
```

**Common Mistakes:**
- Overlapping categories
- Missing edge cases
- Forcing MECE when not appropriate

#### Pyramid Principle
> Structure communication from conclusion to detail

**SCQA Framework:**
- **S**ituation: Context everyone agrees on
- **C**omplication: The problem or change
- **Q**uestion: The question this raises
- **A**nswer: Your recommendation

**Slide Structure:**
1. Governing thought (the "so what")
2. Key line points (supporting arguments)
3. Evidence (data, analysis)

### General Strategy Frameworks

#### Porter's Five Forces
> Industry attractiveness analysis

1. **Threat of New Entrants**
   - Barriers to entry
   - Capital requirements
   - Regulatory hurdles

2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers**
   - Supplier concentration
   - Switching costs
   - Substitute inputs

3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers**
   - Buyer concentration
   - Price sensitivity
   - Switching costs

4. **Threat of Substitutes**
   - Alternative solutions
   - Price-performance tradeoffs
   - Switching costs

5. **Competitive Rivalry**
   - Number of competitors
   - Industry growth
   - Exit barriers

### Executive Communication

#### The Elevator Pitch Structure
```
1. Context (10 seconds)
   "We're evaluating [Company] for acquisition..."

2. Problem/Opportunity (15 seconds)
   "They have strong product-market fit but infrastructure gaps..."

3. Recommendation (15 seconds)
   "We recommend [action] to capture [value]..."

4. Ask (10 seconds)
   "We need your approval to proceed with [next step]..."
```

#### Managing Up
- Lead with answer
- Provide 3 options (with recommendation)
- Anticipate objections
- Bring solutions, not just problems
