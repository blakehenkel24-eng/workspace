# Acceptable Use Policy

**Last Updated:** February 5, 2026  
**Effective Date:** February 5, 2026

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") outlines the rules and guidelines for using SlideTheory's AI-powered presentation slide generation platform and related services ("Services"). By using our Services, you agree to comply with this AUP.

Violation of this AUP may result in suspension or termination of your account, removal of content, and potential legal action.

## 2. Prohibited Activities

### 2.1 Illegal Activities
You may not use SlideTheory for any illegal purposes, including but not limited to:

- Violating any local, state, national, or international law or regulation
- Fraud, money laundering, or financial crimes
- Trafficking in illegal goods or services
- Terrorism or terrorist financing
- Human trafficking or exploitation
- Weapons trafficking or manufacturing instructions
- Drug manufacturing or distribution
- Cybercrime, hacking, or unauthorized access to systems

### 2.2 Harmful Content
You may not generate, upload, or distribute content that:

- Promotes violence, physical harm, or self-harm
- Contains graphic violence, gore, or torture
- Depicts sexual violence or non-consensual sexual content
- Contains child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or exploits minors
- Promotes eating disorders or dangerous behaviors
- Contains instructions for creating weapons, explosives, or dangerous materials
- Promotes dangerous challenges or stunts

### 2.3 Hate Speech and Discrimination
You may not generate content that:

- Attacks or demeans individuals or groups based on protected characteristics
- Promotes hatred against people based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or national origin
- Contains slurs, stereotypes, or dehumanizing language
- Promotes discrimination or segregation
- Glorifies hate groups or extremist ideologies

### 2.4 Harassment and Abuse
You may not use SlideTheory to:

- Harass, bully, or intimidate individuals or groups
- Doxx or publish private information without consent
- Stalk or threaten others
- Send unsolicited communications (spam)
- Impersonate others without authorization
- Create content intended to shame or humiliate

### 2.5 Intellectual Property Violations
You may not:

- Infringe copyrights, trademarks, patents, or trade secrets
- Use proprietary content without authorization
- Remove or alter copyright notices or watermarks
- Distribute pirated content or software
- Use trademarked logos or branding without permission

### 2.6 Misinformation and Deception
You may not generate content that:

- Contains demonstrably false information presented as fact
- Constitutes defamation or libel
- Impersonates public figures or organizations
- Creates fake news or propaganda intended to deceive
- Spreads conspiracy theories harmful to public health or safety
- Creates fake documents or credentials

### 2.7 Privacy Violations
You may not:

- Upload personal data of others without lawful basis and consent
- Generate content that exposes private information
- Use the Services to conduct surveillance without authorization
- Collect data about individuals without disclosure
- Violate applicable privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### 2.8 Security Violations
You may not:

- Attempt to access unauthorized areas of the platform
- Interfere with platform functionality or other users' access
- Distribute malware, viruses, or malicious code
- Conduct denial-of-service attacks
- Scrape or crawl the Services without authorization
- Reverse engineer or decompile our software
- Circumvent security measures or rate limits

### 2.9 Commercial Misuse
You may not:

- Resell SlideTheory services without authorization
- Use the Services to create competing products
- Exceed usage limits through multiple accounts
- Use automated systems (bots) to generate slides at scale
- Share account credentials with unauthorized users
- Use free tiers for commercial redistribution

### 2.10 Professional Services Misrepresentation
You may not use SlideTheory to:

- Create content purporting to be professional legal, medical, or financial advice
- Generate fake credentials or certifications
- Create misleading investment materials or securities offerings
- Produce content that could be mistaken for official government documents

## 3. Content Guidelines

### 3.1 Professional Standards
SlideTheory is designed for professional presentation creation. Content should:

- Be appropriate for business and educational settings
- Maintain professional standards of quality and accuracy
- Respect diverse audiences and cultural sensitivities
- Follow industry-standard ethical guidelines

### 3.2 Accuracy and Verification
You are responsible for:

- Verifying facts and data in generated content
- Ensuring citations and references are accurate
- Reviewing AI-generated content for errors
- Not presenting AI-generated content as human-created without disclosure when required

### 3.3 AI Transparency
When using AI-generated content:

- Disclose AI assistance when required by context or regulation
- Do not misrepresent AI-generated slides as original human work where transparency is expected
- Understand that AI may produce inaccurate or biased content

## 5. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 5.1 Content Review
SlideTheory reserves the right to:

- Review content for compliance with this AUP
- Use automated systems to detect violations
- Remove content that violates this policy
- Report illegal activity to appropriate authorities

### 5.2 Enforcement Actions
Violations may result in:

| Violation Level | Response |
|-----------------|----------|
| Minor/First-time | Warning, content removal |
| Moderate | Temporary suspension (7-30 days) |
| Serious | Account termination, content deletion |
| Illegal | Immediate termination, law enforcement referral |

### 5.3 Appeals
Users may appeal enforcement actions by contacting support@slidetheory.com with:
- Explanation of the situation
- Evidence supporting the appeal
- Commitment to future compliance

Appeals will be reviewed within 5 business days.
